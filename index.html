Topic 1.1

Simple Present Tense

Used for habits, daily routines, and general facts — things that happen always, often, or regularly.

🔁 Habits 📅 Routines 🌍 General Facts
✔️

Affirmative Sentences

Positive Form
Structure
Subject (I/You/We/They) + base verb  |  Subject (He/She/It) + base verb + s/es
  • I manage social media accounts. — "manage" is base verb with "I"
  • He writes reports for the manager. — adds -s for "he"
  • She leads the marketing team. — adds -s for "she"
  • I lead a team of five developers.
  • I manage multiple projects at the same time.
✖️

Negative Sentences

Negative Form
Structure
Subject + do/does not + base verb
  • I do not miss deadlines.
  • She does not work on weekends.
  • They do not handle client communication.
❓

Question Form

Interrogative
Structure
Do/Does + Subject + base verb + ?
  • Do you manage deadlines efficiently?
  • Does he lead any projects?
  • Do they attend client meetings regularly?
⚠️

Common Mistakes & Corrections

Avoid These Errors
❌ Wrong✅ CorrectRule
"He manage the team." "He manages the team." He/She/It needs +s/es on verb
"She don't attend meetings." "She doesn't attend meetings." Use "doesn't" for He/She/It
💡 Daily-use verbs: manage, lead, attend, prepare, handle, communicate

⭐ Final Takeaways

  • Simple Present = Habit + Truth + Routine
  • Use correct verb form with subjects — He/She/It always takes +s/es
  • Example: "She works from home on Mondays."
  • Practice by speaking about your real work/study routine daily

Topic 1.2

Present Continuous Tense

Describes actions happening at the moment of speaking, or temporary/future plans.

🎙 Happening Now 📅 Temporary Plans
✔️

Affirmative Sentences

Positive Form
Structure
Subject + am/is/are + verb+ing
Use: am (I) · is (he/she/it) · are (you/we/they)
  • I am researching the company.
  • She is preparing for the HR round.
  • They are reviewing your application.
  • She is updating her resume.
  • They are discussing the job opening.
✖️

Negative Sentences

Negative Form
Structure
Subject + am/is/are + not + verb+ing
  • I am not attending the walk-in today.
  • He is not applying for that role.
  • We are not waiting for the second interview.
💡 Tip: Use contractions — isn't, aren't, I'm not
❓

Question Form

Interrogative
Structure
Am/Is/Are + Subject + verb+ing + ?
  • Are you preparing for the technical round?
  • Is she attending the interview today?
  • Am I answering your question clearly?
💡 Tip: Respond briefly — "Yes, I am." / "No, she isn't."
⚠️

Common Mistakes & Corrections

Avoid These Errors
❌ Wrong✅ Correct
"She is go to the interview.""She is going to the interview."
"They are prepare their answers.""They are preparing their answers."

⭐ Final Takeaways

  • Use Present Continuous to sound natural in conversations: "I am working on my portfolio right now."
  • Focus on am/is/are + verb+ing for accurate sentences
  • Practice with real job search or interview scenarios to build fluency

Topic 1.3

Present Perfect Tense

Describes actions completed at an unspecified time — connecting the past with the present moment.

🔗 Past → Present✅ Completed Actions
✔️

Affirmative Sentences

Positive Form
Structure
Subject + have/has + past participle
Use "has" for he/she/it  ·  Use "have" for I/you/we/they
  • I have prepared for all the interview rounds.
  • She has joined a new company.
  • They have completed the onboarding process.
  • I have updated my resume.
✖️

Negative Sentences

Negative Form
Structure
Subject + have/has not + past participle
  • I have not received the offer letter yet.
  • He has not cleared the final round.
  • We have not heard back from HR.
💡 Tip: Use haven't/hasn't in spoken English
❓

Question Form

Interrogative
Structure
Have/Has + Subject + past participle + ?
  • Have you prepared for the interview?
  • Has she joined the company already?
  • Have they sent the feedback email?
💡 Tip: Answer with — "Yes, I have." / "No, he hasn't."
⚠️

Common Mistakes & Corrections

❌ Wrong✅ Correct
"I have finish the assignment.""I have finished the assignment."
"He has went to the interview.""He has gone to the interview."
💡 Common verbs: applied, attended, completed, sent, prepared, joined

⭐ Final Takeaways

  • Use Present Perfect to highlight recent achievements: "I have completed several training courses this year."
  • Focus on have/has + past participle — avoid common verb form errors
  • Use it to talk about past actions with present relevance

Topic 1.4

Simple Past Tense

Actions that started and finished in the past — past roles, tasks, or interview experiences.

📖 Completed🕐 Specific Time
✔️

Affirmative Sentences

Positive Form
Structure
Subject + verb (past form)
Regular verbs: add -ed  ·  Irregular verbs: change form (e.g. go → went)
  • I prepared thoroughly for the interview.
  • She attended a training session last Monday.
  • They offered me the position.
  • I attended an interview last week.
✖️

Negative Sentences

Negative Form
Structure
Subject + did not + base verb
  • I did not miss the deadline.
  • She didn't attend the second round.
  • They did not shortlist my application.
❓

Question Form

Interrogative
Structure
Did + Subject + base verb + ?
  • Did you submit your resume on time?
  • Did she join the meeting yesterday?
  • Did they ask about your last role?
💡 Answer with "Yes, I did." / "No, I didn't."
⚠️

Common Mistakes & Corrections

❌ Wrong✅ Correct
"She didn't went to the interview.""She didn't go to the interview."
"I did not explained my project.""I did not explain my project."
💡 Useful verbs: applied, prepared, spoke, joined, answered, attended

⭐ Final Takeaways

  • Use Simple Past to describe completed actions: "I completed the project last week."
  • Focus on verb forms — especially irregular ones: "She went to the interview."
  • Strong storytelling starts with clear, well-structured sentences

Topic 1.5

Past Continuous Tense

An action that was happening at a specific time in the past — sets background context for another event.

⏳ In Progress🖼 Background Context
✔️

Affirmative Sentences

Positive Form
Structure
Subject + was/were + verb+ing
  • I was preparing for the interview when I got the call.
  • She was printing her resume while I was waiting.
  • They were discussing the next round during the break.
  • At 10 AM, I was updating my resume.
✖️

Negative Sentences

Negative Form
Structure
Subject + was/were + not + verb+ing
  • I was not expecting a group discussion.
  • They weren't reviewing my resume carefully.
  • He wasn't preparing for the final round.
💡 Wasn't = was not  ·  Weren't = were not
❓

Question Form

Interrogative
Structure
Was/Were + Subject + verb+ing + ?
  • Were you reading the job description last night?
  • Was she working on her portfolio at the time?
  • Were they interviewing many candidates that day?
💡

When & Why We Use It

Usage Guide
  • Background action: "I was writing my cover letter when the recruiter called."
  • Specific time: "At 10 AM, I was updating my resume."
  • Story background: "The HR was checking her notes while I was speaking."

⭐ Final Takeaways

  • Use Past Continuous to describe what was happening at a specific time: "I was reviewing the job description when the phone rang."
  • Focus on was/were + verb+ing for clear, accurate sentences
  • Practice with real interview-day situations to build confidence

Topic 1.6

Past Perfect Tense

One action completed before another action in the past — used to sequence two past events clearly.

🕰 Before Another Past📋 Sequenced Events
✔️

Affirmative Sentences

Positive Form
Structure
Subject + had + past participle
  • I had researched the company before the interview.
  • I had finished my elevator pitch before the recruiter called.
  • She had submitted her application before the job posting closed.
  • By the time I arrived, they had already started the interview.
✖️

Negative Sentences

Negative Form
Structure
Subject + had not + past participle
  • I had not updated my resume before the interview.
  • They hadn't finished reviewing my portfolio when I arrived.
  • He hadn't prepared his introduction before the interview.
💡 Hadn't is the contracted form of had not
❓

Question Form

Interrogative
Structure
Had + Subject + past participle + ?
  • Had you completed the form before the interview?
  • Had she reviewed the job description?
  • Had they asked all the candidates by the time I arrived?

⭐ Final Takeaways

  • Use Past Perfect to show that one action was completed before another: "I had completed the assessment before the interview started."
  • Focus on "had + past participle" for correct formation
  • Practice in job-related situations: "She had prepared her portfolio before meeting the recruiter."

Topic 1.7

Simple Future Tense

Actions that will happen — predictions, plans, or intentions for the future.

🚀 Plans🔮 Predictions💬 Intentions
✔️

Affirmative Sentences

Positive Form
Structure
Subject + will + base verb
  • I will send my application by the end of today.
  • They will discuss the final round tomorrow.
  • She will call me after reviewing my resume.
  • I will prepare my portfolio before the interview.
✖️

Negative Sentences

Negative Form
Structure
Subject + will not (won't) + base verb
  • I will not be available for an interview tomorrow.
  • They won't contact me until next week.
  • She will not attend the meeting if she's sick.
💡 Use won't as the contraction of will not for a more natural sentence
❓

Question Form

Interrogative
Structure
Will + Subject + base verb + ?
  • Will you attend the interview next week?
  • Will they inform you about the results today?
  • Will she ask you for references during the interview?

⭐ Final Takeaways

  • Use Simple Future to talk about actions that will happen: "I will prepare for the interview this afternoon."
  • Focus on "will" + base form to express future intentions or predictions
  • Practice with real interview situations: "They will announce the results soon."

Topic 1.8

Future Continuous Tense

An action that will be happening at a specific time in the future — ongoing future activities.

🌐 Ongoing Future📅 Planned Events
✔️

Affirmative Sentences

Positive Form
Structure
Subject + will be + verb+ing
  • I will be preparing for the interview when you call.
  • She will be attending a meeting while I'm interviewing.
  • They will be discussing the candidates throughout the day.
  • I will be completing my interview at 3 PM tomorrow.
✖️

Negative Sentences

Negative Form
Structure
Subject + will not (won't) be + verb+ing
  • I will not be available for a call during the interview.
  • They won't be discussing the results until next week.
  • She won't be taking the test on Friday.
❓

Question Form

Interrogative
Structure
Will + Subject + be + verb+ing + ?
  • Will you be attending the interview next week?
  • Will they be giving you a presentation task?
  • Will she be meeting with the hiring manager?

⭐ Final Takeaways

  • Use Future Continuous for actions happening at a specific future time: "I will be working on my resume tomorrow afternoon."
  • Focus on "will be + verb-ing" for actions in progress at a future time
  • Practice with job-related situations: "They will be interviewing candidates throughout the day."
